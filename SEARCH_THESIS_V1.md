# Search Thesis V1

## Position

This project should not compete as a generic PFAS explainer or a broad "best filter" affiliate site.

It should compete as a decision engine for households trying to answer:

- What is my next action?
- What direct evidence matters first?
- Do I need testing, utility interpretation, or only a certified point-of-use review?

## Persona Synthesis

### Search Strategist

- Head terms like `PFAS water filter` are real but crowded.
- The better wedge is decision-intent mid-tail.
- Winning pages should resolve a specific household fork, not repeat a generic PFAS summary.

### Domain Skeptic

- The project is only credible when direct evidence outranks geography, fear, and product marketing.
- Utility dossiers and state well guides are the strongest trust surface because they tie action to named source records.

### Monetization Realist

- Revenue is downstream of trust.
- The site should not behave like a roundup-first affiliate property.
- Product pages should be examples inside an evidence-backed route, not the editorial center of gravity.

### Editorial Lead

- Guides need enough depth to stand on their own and enough linked evidence to justify indexing.
- "Who/How/Why" transparency matters more here than design polish.

## Query Clusters

### Cluster A: Routing Split

Examples:

- `public water vs private well pfas`
- `pfas public water private well difference`
- `private well vs utility water pfas`

Goal:

- Push users into the correct evidence system before product comparison.

Why it matters:

- Strong fit for the engine's first decision boundary.
- High trust value.
- Not inherently commercial, but it improves downstream product fit.

### Cluster B: Decision Timing

Examples:

- `test first vs filter first pfas`
- `should i test for pfas before buying a filter`
- `pfas test first or filter first`

Goal:

- Frame evidence collection as the first action, not shopping.

Why it matters:

- Helps the site differentiate from vendor and thin affiliate content.
- Supports later product recommendations without looking premature.

### Cluster C: Utility Interpretation

Examples:

- `how to read ccr pfas`
- `how to read utility water report pfas`
- `ccr pfas interpretation`

Goal:

- Convert utility documents into constrained household decisions.

Why it matters:

- This is one of the clearest SERP wedges because official sources explain CCRs, but most users still cannot act on them.

### Cluster D: Certification Comparison

Examples:

- `nsf 53 vs 58 pfas`
- `which certification for pfas filters`
- `pfas certification meaning`

Goal:

- Move the user from badge confusion to claim-level reading.

Why it matters:

- Strong commercial adjacency.
- Good bridge from education to product examples.

### Cluster E: Treatment Scope Comparison

Examples:

- `under sink vs whole house pfas filter`
- `do i need whole house pfas filter`
- `whole house vs under sink pfas`

Goal:

- Keep point-of-use visible as the likely first serious treatment class.

Why it matters:

- High commercial intent.
- High risk of fear-driven oversell.
- Good opportunity for the product to win by being more disciplined than vendor content.

### Cluster F: Ownership Cost

Examples:

- `pfas filter annual cost`
- `pfas filter maintenance cost`
- `pfas filter replacement cost`

Goal:

- Make annual burden part of the decision, not a hidden detail after emotional commitment.

Why it matters:

- Strong money-intent query set.
- Good fit for product comparison without becoming a thin roundup.

## Pages To Avoid For Now

- Broad `best PFAS filter` pages as the main acquisition wedge
- City-by-city or ZIP-like doorway pages without named direct evidence
- State pages without lab guidance and benchmark context
- Product pages that cannot show claim-level support

## Monetization Thesis

- Product monetization should happen inside result pages, guide examples, and comparison surfaces.
- Certified point-of-use products are the cleanest first monetization layer.
- Whole-house monetization should stay constrained because trust damage is more expensive than short-term upside.

## Quality Bar

Before a page type expands, it should show:

- direct source linkage
- last verified date
- decision usefulness
- route-specific value that cannot be replaced by a generic PFAS explainer

## Next Expansion Bias

Bias the next growth rounds toward:

1. more public-water dossiers
2. more certified point-of-use products
3. stronger guide-to-dossier and guide-to-product connections
4. freshness and noindex automation

Do not bias the next rounds toward final design polish or broad, weak content coverage.
